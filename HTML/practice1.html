Malala Yousfzai

The Afghan Jeanne D'Arc

Malala was a strong advocate for girls' education, which started when she was just a young girl.
As she got older, she started giving memorable speeches at an attempt to convince other nations to start focusing on womens' rights and education.
Two years after getting shot, she became the youngest person to win a Nobel Peace Prize.

  • Malala is the youngest recipient of the Nobel Peace Prize.
  • She has been advocating for girls' education since she was 11 years old.
  • She was awarded 1.1 million dollars for her work.
  • She published her first memoir in 2014, as it was also a best seller.
  • Malala was only 15 years old when she was shot by the Taliban.
